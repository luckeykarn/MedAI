Influenza (Flu)

High Risk Viral

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. It can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death. The best way to prevent the flu is by getting a flu vaccine each year.

Symptoms of flu can include fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, headache, chills and fatigue. Some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children than adults.

Warning: People at high risk of serious flu complications include young children, adults 65 years of age and older, pregnant women, and people with certain medical conditions.
Common Symptoms
Fever (100°F or higher) Cough (often severe) Sore throat Runny or stuffy nose Muscle or body aches Headaches Fatigue (tiredness) Vomiting and diarrhea (more common in children)
Emergency Warning Signs
Seek immediate medical care if you experience:
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest or abdomen
  • Persistent dizziness, confusion, inability to arouse
  • Seizures
  • Severe muscle pain
How Influenza Spreads

Flu viruses spread mainly by tiny droplets made when people with flu cough, sneeze or talk. These droplets can land in the mouths or noses of people who are nearby. Less often, a person might get flu by touching a surface or object that has flu virus on it and then touching their own mouth, nose or possibly their eyes.

Types of Influenza Viruses
  • Influenza A (most common, causes epidemics)
  • Influenza B (less severe, seasonal)
  • Influenza C (mild symptoms)
Risk Factors
  • Age (young children and elderly)
  • Weakened immune system
  • Chronic illnesses (asthma, diabetes, heart disease)
  • Pregnancy
  • Obesity
Medications
Antiviral Drugs

Prescription antiviral drugs can treat flu illness. They can make illness milder and shorten the time you are sick.

  • Oseltamivir (Tamiflu®)
  • Zanamivir (Relenza®)
  • Peramivir (Rapivab®)
Note: Antibiotics don't work against flu viruses.
Home Remedies
  • Get plenty of rest
  • Drink clear fluids (water, broth)
  • Use a humidifier or saline nasal spray
  • Gargle with salt water for sore throat
  • Use honey or lozenges for cough
Prevention
The best way to prevent flu is by getting vaccinated each year.
  • Get a flu vaccine every year
  • Avoid close contact with sick people
  • Wash hands frequently with soap and water
  • Cover coughs and sneezes
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces
Quick Facts
Incubation Period

1-4 days (average 2 days)

Duration

3-7 days (may last longer in some cases)

Contagious Period

1 day before symptoms to 5-7 days after

Diagnosis

Rapid influenza diagnostic tests (RIDTs), RT-PCR
